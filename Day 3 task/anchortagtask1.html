Ice Cream 

The meaning of the name ice cream varies from one country to another. In some countries, such as the
United States,ice cream applies only to a specific variety, and most governments regulate the commercial use of the various terms 
according to the relative quantities of the main ingredients, notably the amount of cream.[1] Products that do not 
meet the criteria to be called ice cream are sometimes labelled "frozen dairy dessert" instead.[2] In other countries,
such as Italy and Argentina, one word is used for all variants.


Ice cream may be served in dishes, eaten with a spoon, or licked from edible wafer 
ice cream cones held by the hands as finger food. 
Ice cream may be served with other desserts—such as cake or pie or
 used as an ingredient in cold dishes—like ice cream floats, sundaes,
 milkshakes, and ice cream cakes—or in baked items such as Baked Alaska.
